Auction said no rust, and it really didn't have any rust. Later I found out that they had cheaply fixed a few places, so they rusted out after six months. But, the fix up's are destined for another blog post :)

Here is the set up I started competing with...

### Audio, DSP

After my friend convinced me to actually start competing, we both bought Audison Bit One's as the bases for our cars stereo systems.

The DSP has 6 inputs for low-level (RCA), optical input, high-level input and 8 output channels.
All packaged in a nice relatively small black chassis, with 31-band EQ 

{{< img src="audison_bitone.jpg" alt="Shiny." >}}

Channel configuration was active two-way front stage with mono sub woofer.

### Audio, amplifiers

I bought another SPL Dynamics ICE 150.4 amp to run the following configuration:
- 1 channel (4ohm) per tweeter
- 2 channels bridged (4ohm) per mid
- 2 channels bridged (4ohm) to sub woofer

Then I built a nice amp rack to hold the amps and bit one, and hide all the wires.

{{< img src="amp_rack.jpg" alt="Amps installed." >}}

PSST... Can you see the mistake made with power wiring?

### Audio, wiring

All the power to the trunk was handled by 50mm2 copper cable, protected by a 60amp ANL fuse installed to the fusebox in engine bay.
Picture to come

To use a two way active front-end on this particular car, we had to run wires for mids and highs from the trunk.
1.5mm2 speaker wires were pulled trough the front door rubbers.

{{< img src="door_wiring.jpg" alt="Door wiring." >}}

### Sound, highs

For highs, I bought [Hertz Mille Legend ML280.3](https://www.hertzaudiovideo.com/car-audio-tweeters-mille-legend-ml280_3/) tweeters.

And wow, I wasn't sure what I was expecting of them, but they blew all of them away...
Mind you, this was the first time I bought actual "high-end" car audio components, so I did not expect them to be so nicely crafted and packaged, and they also sounded nice.

I would describe the sound being a bit "clinical", but it is perfect for the music I normally listen to (mostly electronic).

{{< img src="hertz_ml280_box.jpg" alt="ML280 packagin." >}}
{{< img src="ML280Graf.jpg" alt="ML280 Frequency response diagram." >}}

### Sound, mids

Thinking back, I have no idea what I thought that this combination of "high-end" highs and these mids would do.
But, my excuse at the time was tight budget, so I bought [these Kicx LL 6.5 mid-woofers](https://www.autoviihde.com/2012625.html).

{{< img src="kicx_door.jpg" alt="Kicx installed." >}}

### Sound, bass

For bass, I ditched the JVC 12" sub, for a prefab Ground Zero 10" ported sub.

It was a OK sub, but you could hear all the hours played with it, as the bass lacked "punch". Low-end extension was "perfect", as the sub went easily under 25hz, but over 60, no life there. The sub probably had over 1000 (loud) hours played before I got it, as it was installed in my friends "moped-car". 

{{< img src="ground_zero_trunk.jpg" alt="Ground Zero installed." >}}

### Sound, deadening

For the first competition, I only deadened the trunk, and front doors. Materials used were Kicx 1.6mm foil-covered bitumen, STP Biplast 10 closed cell foam, applied generously :)

{{< img src="trunk_kicx.jpg" alt="Kicx bitumen installed." >}}
{{< img src="trunk_stp.jpg" alt="STP Biplast installed." >}}

# Results...

So, all ready for the first competition, Backwoods Cruising, Kajaani!

The first results were, for a lack of better word, "shit". Mid and bass was horrible, but the highs had some hope.
Also, I lost a few install points with the fuses, and no pictures of few components.

[Here are the scores in PDF format](http://emmafinland.com/wp-content/uploads/2019/05/18-05-2019-Backwoods-Cruising.pdf), look for SQ E-Cat 3000 (And yes, I also started in the wrong class...).

But, I also learned a lot that day. The bad results, but actually good feedback from the judges gave me even more inspiration to continue building the car up for some real action.
I also learned, that you can't tune your car for a better sound, if you don't know what to look for. I sat in a few SQ Master-class cars, and learned a lot about music that day.
